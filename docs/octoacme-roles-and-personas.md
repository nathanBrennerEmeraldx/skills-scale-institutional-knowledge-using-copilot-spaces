# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers oversee deployment planning and release execution. They coordinate with QA, Development, and Product teams to ensure smooth, safe releases to production and are responsible for go/no-go decisions.

### Responsibilities
- Plan and schedule releases, including release windows and deployment timing
- Coordinate release activities across development, QA, and operations teams
- Make go/no-go decisions based on readiness criteria and risk assessment
- Ensure deployment checklists and runbooks are followed
- Monitor post-deployment health and coordinate rollback if needed
- Maintain release documentation and post-mortem analysis

### Goals
- Ensure safe, reliable deployments with minimal downtime
- Minimize production incidents and deployment failures
- Maintain clear release schedules and stakeholder communication

### Typical Communication
- Release planning meetings with Dev, QA, and Product
- Go/no-go checkpoint meetings before each release
- Post-deployment status updates to stakeholders
- Release retrospectives and incident reviews

### Interaction Points
- **Project Manager:** Aligns on project milestones and dependencies; coordinates stakeholder communication
- **QA Lead:** Collaborates on release readiness, test coverage, and acceptance sign-off
- **Developers:** Reviews code completion, deployment requirements, and rollback procedures
- **Product Manager:** Validates feature completeness and prioritizes release scope

---

## Risk Owner

### Role Summary
Risk Owners identify, log, and manage project risks throughout the project lifecycle. They collaborate with Project Managers on mitigation actions and communicate risk updates to stakeholders.

### Responsibilities
- Identify and document project risks in the Risk Register
- Assess impact and likelihood of identified risks
- Develop and track mitigation plans and contingencies
- Monitor risk indicators and update risk status regularly
- Escalate critical risks to Project Manager and stakeholders
- Facilitate risk review sessions during project milestones

### Goals
- Proactively identify and mitigate risks before they impact delivery
- Maintain accurate, up-to-date risk documentation
- Enable informed decision-making through clear risk communication

### Typical Communication
- Weekly risk reviews with Project Manager
- Risk status updates in stakeholder reports
- Escalation notices for high-priority risks
- Risk retrospectives and lessons learned

### Interaction Points
- **Project Manager:** Primary partnership for risk tracking, mitigation planning, and escalation
- **Developers:** Identifies technical risks and validates feasibility of mitigations
- **Product Manager:** Assesses impact on product scope and timeline
- **Executive Sponsor:** Escalates risks requiring organizational resources or strategic decisions

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
QA Leads supervise all quality assurance activities, establish test protocols, and serve as the primary point of contact for bug triage. They work closely with Release Managers and Development teams to ensure quality standards are met.

### Responsibilities
- Define test strategy and quality standards for projects
- Establish and maintain test protocols, test plans, and acceptance criteria
- Lead bug triage and prioritization efforts
- Coordinate testing activities across manual and automated testing
- Sign off on release readiness from a quality perspective
- Track quality metrics and identify improvement opportunities

### Goals
- Ensure features meet acceptance criteria and quality standards
- Minimize production defects and customer-impacting bugs
- Maintain comprehensive test coverage and documentation

### Typical Communication
- Daily collaboration with developers on feature testing
- Weekly quality metrics review with Project Manager
- Release readiness sign-off meetings with Release Manager
- Bug triage sessions and test planning meetings

### Interaction Points
- **Release Manager:** Collaborates on release readiness criteria and go/no-go decisions
- **Developers:** Partners on test strategy, bug reproduction, and quality improvements
- **Product Manager:** Validates acceptance criteria and prioritizes quality improvements
- **Project Manager:** Reports quality status, risks, and resource needs

---

## Communications Coordinator

### Role Summary
Communications Coordinators ensure consistent updates are shared with internal and external stakeholders, manage project documentation, and support cross-team information flow throughout the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Develop and execute communication plans for projects
- Maintain project documentation and ensure accessibility
- Coordinate stakeholder updates and announcements
- Facilitate information sharing across cross-functional teams
- Manage communication channels and distribution lists
- Support change management and user adoption communications

### Goals
- Ensure stakeholders receive timely, accurate project information
- Maintain clear, consistent messaging across all audiences
- Enable effective collaboration through well-organized documentation

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates to stakeholder distribution lists
- Release announcements and change notifications
- Documentation updates and knowledge sharing sessions
- Cross-team sync meetings and information requests

### Interaction Points
- **Project Manager:** Partners on stakeholder updates, status reports, and escalation communications
- **Product Manager:** Translates product vision and feature updates for different audiences
- **Release Manager:** Coordinates release announcements and deployment communications
- **Executive Sponsor:** Prepares executive-level updates and strategic communications

---

## Executive Sponsor

### Role Summary
Executive Sponsors provide strategic oversight for projects, secure organizational resources, and resolve escalated issues. They serve as the liaison between project teams and executive leadership.

### Responsibilities
- Provide strategic direction and alignment with organizational goals
- Secure funding, headcount, and other organizational resources
- Remove organizational blockers and resolve escalated issues
- Review and approve major project decisions and scope changes
- Champion the project at executive and board levels
- Ensure project outcomes align with business strategy

### Goals
- Ensure project success delivers on strategic business objectives
- Provide organizational support and remove high-level impediments
- Maintain executive visibility and alignment

### Typical Communication
- Monthly project reviews with Project Manager and Product Manager
- Executive briefings on project status, risks, and decisions
- Ad-hoc escalation discussions for critical issues
- Strategic planning sessions and budget reviews

### Interaction Points
- **Project Manager:** Receives regular updates, provides guidance on escalated issues and resource needs
- **Product Manager:** Aligns on strategic priorities and validates business outcomes
- **Risk Owner:** Reviews critical risks and approves major mitigation strategies
- **Communications Coordinator:** Reviews key stakeholder messaging and strategic announcements
